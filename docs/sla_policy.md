# Service Level Agreement (SLA) Policy

- No acceptable workaround

Examples:

- Webhook failures
- Broken exports
- Billing failures
- Severe performance degradation

Target Response Time: 1 hour
Target Resolution Time: 8 hours

---

## P3 / Medium

Definition:

- Partial feature malfunction
- Non-critical workflow issues
- Workaround available

Examples:

- UI bugs
- Delayed reports
- Import/export inconsistencies

Target Response Time: 4 hours
Target Resolution Time: 24 hours

---

## P4 / Low

Definition:

- Cosmetic issues
- Informational requests
- Feature requests
- General guidance

Examples:

- Documentation clarification
- Product questions
- Minor UI alignment issues

Target Response Time: 8 hours
Target Resolution Time: 72 hours

---

# SLA Breach Rules

A ticket is considered breached when:

- first response exceeds SLA target
- resolution exceeds SLA target
- customer update cadence is missed

Repeated SLA breaches for enterprise customers must be escalated to operations leadership.

---

# Enterprise Customer Handling

Enterprise customers receive:

- priority queue routing
- dedicated escalation handling
- accelerated SLA targets
- direct manager visibility
- executive reporting for critical incidents

---

# Business Hours

Standard support business hours:

- Monday to Friday
- 9 AM to 6 PM UTC

Critical incidents are handled 24/7.
