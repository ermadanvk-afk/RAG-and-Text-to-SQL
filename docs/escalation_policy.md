# Escalation Workflow
- ticket unassigned for 30 minutes
- first response SLA at risk
- customer reports business disruption

Escalated To:

- L2 Support Team
- Team Lead

---

## Level 2 Escalation

Triggered when:

- ticket unresolved after first escalation
- customer requests management involvement
- repeated reopen events occur
- multiple users impacted

Escalated To:

- Engineering Team
- Operations Manager

---

## Level 3 Escalation

Triggered when:

- major outage occurs
- security incident confirmed
- SLA breach exceeds 24 hours
- executive customers impacted

Escalated To:

- Incident Commander
- Security Team
- Engineering Leadership
- Executive Stakeholders

---

# Mandatory Escalation Conditions

The following conditions require immediate escalation:

- P1 tickets
- suspected data breach
- authentication failures affecting multiple customers
- payment processing outage
- infrastructure outage

---

# Escalation Communication Rules

Support agents must:

- provide updates every 30 minutes for P1 tickets
- document escalation reason clearly
- attach investigation findings
- notify affected customers during outages

---

# Escalation Closure

An escalation can be closed only when:

- customer confirms resolution
- monitoring stabilizes
- RCA has been documented for major incidents
